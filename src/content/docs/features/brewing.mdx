Brewing lets you create custom drinks using cauldrons, barrels, and special ingredients. Drinks can ferment, age, and even affect players when consumed.

---

## Fermenting

Fermenting is the first step in creating a brew.

### Steps:

1. Place a cauldron over a heat source (fire, campfire, lava, etc.)
2. Fill it with water
3. Add ingredients by right-clicking the cauldron
4. Wait for the mixture to ferment
5. Fill glass bottles with the brew

### Checking Progress
Right-click the cauldron with a clock to see how long it has been fermenting.

---

## Aging

After fermenting, drinks can be aged to improve or change their quality.

### How it works:

- Place bottled brews into a barrel
- Each Minecraft day = 1 year of aging
- The type of wood used can affect the final quality

## Sealing

Sealing is used to make brews tradeable and compatible with shop systems.

### How sealing works:

- Brew Sealing Table is used to seal drinks
- Crafted using:
  - 2 Bottles
  - 4 Wooden Planks

### Effects of sealing:

- Makes all similar brews identical for trading
- Removes brew details and metadata
- Prevents further aging or modification

## Getting Sober

Alcohol effects wear off over time, but can also be reduced faster by consuming certain items.

### What happens when drunk:

- Alcohol level decreases over time
- Logging off while very drunk may teleport you to a random location when you return
- If offline for a long time, you may respawn at spawn with no memory of events
- Low-quality drinks may cause hangover effects like:
  - Slowness
  - Hunger
